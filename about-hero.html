Our History

  1. 2005
    Abaris Healthcare was founded in July 2005 by buying out the Rajpur unit of Core Healthcare Ltd. It also purchased CHL’s brands—Corflam, Clomin and Fenac-P.
  2. 2006
    Operations began in Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, Chhattisgarh and Madhya Pradesh. Within one year, Abaris introduced 12 new formulations including third-generation antibiotics and cough syrups.
  3. 2007
    Introduced 4 new formulations and established another unit at Himachal Pradesh (Excise Free Zone). Procured a Rommelag S.V.P. machine for 5 ml and 10 ml ampoules. Expanded distribution to Punjab.
  4. 2008
    Adopted a new formulation for Asthma and Seasonal Allergic Rhinitis.
  5. 2009
    Introduced 5 new formulations to the market.
  6. 2010
    Developed two new IV formulations—Paracetamol, and Ofloxacin & Ornidazole—in 100 ml plastic bottles.
  7. 2011
    Increased parenteral range by adding Rommelag FFS machinery with 1000 ml, 500 ml and 300 ml moulds.
  8. 2012
    Began Abaris domestic business.
  9. 2015
    Received regulatory approvals in Uganda, Kenya, Congo and Nepal.
  10. 2016
    Expansion: SVP Injectable Plant set up as per PIC/s norms (2 ml to 30 ml GV & GA).
  11. 2017
    Continued expansion of SVP Injectable Plant as per PIC/s norms (2 ml to 30 ml GV & GA).
  12. 2018
    Received regulatory approvals in Russia and Ethiopia.

Emerging Business Vistas

In today’s lifestyle there are various diseases affecting large sections of both urban and rural populations. These “lifestyle diseases” drive the need for effective, affordable medicines. Key areas include:

  • Increasing cases of anxiety, stress and depression—driving the use of anti-stress and anxiolytic drugs.
  • Obesity—now a common social and health concern—leading to higher demand for anti-obesity medicines.
  • Population growth—expanding opportunity in the paediatric segment.
  • Increased life expectancy—greater need for geriatric therapies.
  • Cardiac diseases and strokes—rising prevalence in urban life.
  • Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease—an area that demands sincere attention and effort.
  • WHO research indicates ~47% of industrial workers are vulnerable to chronic conditions (e.g., hypertension, cancer) requiring long-term therapies.
Moreover, these lifestyle and chronic diseases guide Abaris Healthcare to deliver effective healthcare solutions and prepare for changing times. We focus on new formulations for this growing market while expanding our parenteral division to cater to domestic and international demand. Our visionary, planned approach inspires us to formulate the best medicines for the welfare of society.
